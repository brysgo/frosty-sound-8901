How to Set Up an AI Chatbot for Your Small Business Website

March 24, 2026

Introduction

Customer service is expensive, especially for small businesses operating with lean teams. You might have a handful of staff handling customer inquiries across email, chat, and social media—and when a question comes in at 2 AM, nobody’s there to answer it.

That’s where AI chatbots come in. A good chatbot can handle 60-80% of routine customer questions automatically—password resets, order tracking, FAQ responses—freeing your team to focus on complex issues that actually need human touch. For small businesses, this means answering more customers with the same headcount, while improving response times from hours to seconds.

But setting up a chatbot isn’t about finding the fanciest AI. It’s about finding the right tool that fits your business model, your budget, and your technical comfort level. I’ve set up chatbots for everything from e-commerce stores to SaaS platforms, and I’ve learned what actually works versus what looks impressive in a demo.

In this guide, I’ll walk you through exactly what to look for in a business chatbot, show you the real tools that small teams actually use, and help you figure out which one makes sense for your specific situation.

What to Look For

Before you look at any specific tool, know what matters:

Easy Integration Without Code Your website probably runs on Shopify, WordPress, Wix, or Squarespace. Your chatbot needs to plug in without hiring a developer. Look for tools that offer simple copy-paste widget installation or native integrations with your platform.

Conversation Routing & Handoff A chatbot isn’t perfect. When a customer’s question is too complex or the chatbot is stuck, it should smoothly hand the conversation to a human team member. This isn’t a bonus feature—it’s essential. Customers hate talking to a bot that can’t help them.

Training on Your Own Data Generic chatbots sound like chatbots. The best ones learn from your actual website content, help documentation, and FAQs. Your customers should feel like they’re talking to someone familiar with your business, not a generic bot.

Multi-Channel Support Do you need chat on your website only? Or also WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, Instagram? Some small businesses get surprising traffic from messaging apps. Pick a tool that covers the channels where your customers actually are.

Reasonable Pricing Structure Chatbot pricing varies wildly. Some charge per conversation, some per active user, some have fixed monthly tiers. For small businesses, a flat monthly fee is usually easier to predict. Watch out for tools that seem cheap until you hit a threshold and the price jumps.

Basic Analytics & Insights You need to know which questions the chatbot is getting wrong, what customers ask most, and when the handoff to humans happens. This helps you improve over time.

Top Tools

Tidio

Tidio is what I recommend to most small businesses just starting out. It’s approachable, affordable, and does the fundamentals well.

The free tier lets you set up a basic chatbot with up to 10 conversations per month—useful for testing, but you’ll outgrow it quickly if you get decent traffic. The Starter plan ($29/month) gives you unlimited conversations and covers most small business needs. You get a visual chatbot builder (no coding required), the ability to train it on your website or upload a knowledge base, and conversations flow to your team’s inbox when needed.

Tidio integrates directly into Shopify, WordPress, Wix, and Squarespace. Installation takes minutes. The interface is clean, and their documentation is actually readable. One advantage: Tidio includes email and live chat in the same platform, so everything lands in one place.

Pricing: Free ($0), Starter ($29/month), Growth ($49/month), Unlimited ($99/month).

Intercom

Intercom is bigger and more powerful, which means it’s also more complex and more expensive. But if you’re scaling beyond the basics, it’s worth considering.

Intercom’s real strength is its unified customer view. Every message, every interaction, every support ticket lives in one profile. Your team can see a customer’s entire history before jumping in. The chatbot learns from your website content, and you can build complex conversation flows with conditional logic.

Setup is straightforward for simple use cases, though more advanced setups require some configuration. Intercom offers a free tier, but it’s limited. The Inbox plan starts at $39/month (when paid annually) and includes essential chatbot features. The real advantage shows up when you scale—Intercom handles high-volume conversations without breaking a sweat.

The downside: Intercom’s pricing can get expensive fast if you add multiple team members or need advanced features. Plan for $100-300/month once you’ve built out your setup.

Pricing: Free ($0, limited), Inbox ($39/month), Standard ($99/month), Plus ($199/month).

Drift

Drift built its entire platform around the idea of “conversational business”—using chat as a primary channel to connect with customers at the right moment.

What sets Drift apart is real-time visitor identification. When someone lands on your pricing page, Drift can trigger a targeted message offering a demo. For SaaS and B2B businesses, this is genuinely useful. The chatbot handles routine questions, but Drift’s real power is proactive engagement.

The platform is slick and well-designed, but it’s positioned more toward growth-stage companies. Pricing starts around $50/month, but you’ll likely spend more to unlock the features that make it worthwhile. Drift works best when you have a dedicated person managing conversations.

Pricing: Starts around $50/month for basic features; enterprise pricing available.

ManyChat

ManyChat specializes in omnichannel automation, particularly for e-commerce and service businesses. If you’re serious about WhatsApp, Instagram, or Facebook Messenger, ManyChat is one of the best options.

The free tier is genuinely useful—you can automate up to 1,000 subscribers across channels. That’s a real tool, not a crippled demo. The $25/month Unlimited plan removes subscriber limits and unlocks advanced automation.

ManyChat’s chatbots can do things like order status updates, appointment confirmations, and promotional broadcasts. For a coffee shop, salon, or e-commerce store, this is powerful stuff. The builder is visual and user-friendly.

The trade-off: ManyChat’s website chat is less sophisticated than dedicated tools like Tidio. If your primary need is website chat only, use Tidio. But if you’re already heavy on WhatsApp or Facebook, ManyChat saves you from managing multiple tools.

Pricing: Free ($0), Unlimited ($25-$75/month depending on annual commitment).

HubSpot Chatbot

HubSpot’s free tier is genuinely generous. You get a chatbot builder, conversational forms, and chat history without paying anything. This matters for cash-strapped startups.

The catch: The free tier is limited to basic features. But it’s a real, working chatbot, not a restricted trial. If you already use HubSpot’s free CRM, adding a chatbot costs nothing and integrates seamlessly.

When you’re ready to upgrade, HubSpot’s Starter plan ($50/month) adds better analytics, more automation, and conversation routing. HubSpot’s strength is that everything integrates—your chatbot, your CRM, your email marketing, your help desk. Everything talks to everything.

Pricing: Free ($0), Starter ($50/month), Professional ($800/month).

Gorgias

Gorgias is built specifically for e-commerce merchants. If you run a Shopify store, Gorgias integrates directly and can look up customer order history, process refunds, and handle shipping questions automatically.

The $10/month Starter plan covers basic automation. For a store handling dozens of daily orders, the ROI is immediate. Gorgias can pull order details and automatically suggest solutions before a human even touches the conversation.

Pricing: Starter ($10/month), Professional ($30/month), Advanced ($100/month).

Who Should Use This

E-commerce on Shopify or WooCommerce? Start with Gorgias ($10/month) or Tidio ($29/month). Both integrate seamlessly and handle order tracking, shipping, and refund questions automatically.

Service business (salon, coaching, cleaning, etc.)? ManyChat ($25/month) if you need appointment confirmations and reminders via WhatsApp. Tidio if you primarily need website chat.

SaaS or B2B software? Drift if you can justify $50+/month for proactive engagement and visitor targeting. Intercom if you’re growing and need powerful team features.

New business on a tight budget? HubSpot’s free chatbot is genuinely solid. Or Tidio’s free tier if you need something simpler than HubSpot’s ecosystem.

Already using HubSpot’s CRM? Their free chatbot is a no-brainer. The integration is seamless, and you’re not paying for another tool.

Getting Started

Step 1: List Your Most Common Questions Before you set up anything, spend an hour documenting the 20-30 questions your customers ask most frequently. Check your email, your support tickets, your social DMs. Your chatbot will handle these first.

Step 2: Choose Your Tool Use the pricing and features above to narrow down to 2-3 options. Most offer free trials—test them with your actual website and actual questions.

Step 3: Install & Configure For most tools, installation takes 10 minutes (copy-paste widget code) or 2 minutes (native integration). Train the chatbot on your FAQ or website content.

Step 4: Build Your First Flows Start simple. Build flows for 3-5 common questions. Set up handoff to a human for anything the bot isn’t sure about. Don’t try to automate everything immediately.

Step 5: Monitor & Improve Check analytics weekly for the first month. What questions is the bot getting wrong? Which handoffs happen most? Use this feedback to improve your responses and fix gaps.

Step 6: Expand (Optional) Once the basics work, add more channels, build more automations, or layer in other tools. But the fundamentals—handling routine questions and routing complex issues to humans—should come first.

Final Recommendation

For most small businesses, Tidio at $29/month is the best starting point. It’s affordable, easy to set up, includes email and live chat alongside chatbot features, and you won’t outgrow it for at least a year. If you run a Shopify store, Gorgias saves you more money than it costs. If you’re WhatsApp-first, ManyChat is your answer.

The key insight: Your first chatbot doesn’t need to be perfect. It needs to exist, handle the easy stuff, and give your team time back. From there, you can iterate, improve, and expand based on what actually happens with your customers.
